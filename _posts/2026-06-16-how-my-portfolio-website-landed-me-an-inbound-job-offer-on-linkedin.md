<p>I want to share a story with you because it completely changed how I view the job search. A while back, I was exhausted from sending out hundreds of resumes into the void. So, I stopped applying. Instead, I spent a weekend building a simple portfolio website and linked it right at the top of my LinkedIn profile in the "Featured" section.</p>

<p>Then, I waited.</p>

<p>Within three weeks, I got a direct message from a recruiter. She didn't ask me to apply for a job. She said, "I saw your portfolio link on your LinkedIn, I loved the case study you posted, and we have a role that seems built for you. Are you open to chatting?" I ended up getting an offer without ever filling out a single application.</p>

<p>Here is why this works so well for you and me: Recruiters are lazy. Well, maybe not lazy, but they are incredibly efficient. When they search LinkedIn for candidates, they see thousands of identical text profiles. But when they spot that little "Featured" link with a custom thumbnail, their eyes naturally dart to it. They click it because it’s easier than reading your summary.</p>

<p>By putting your portfolio front and center on LinkedIn, you turn a passive profile into an inbound lead generator. You stop chasing opportunities and start attracting them. You make it frictionless for talent scouts to see your best work. If you want recruiters to come to you, you have to give them a link worth clicking.</p>
